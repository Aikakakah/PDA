Sol-131 (There’s That Buzzing Bitch…)

Dimmed lights cast long shadows across the narrow hallway that was evac, giving the place an almost ethereal glow. The night cycle had taken hold, and most of the station was quiet, apart from the occasional hum of a borg, machinery whirring in the distance, or the soft footsteps of someone walking through the main hall.

Ramona stood near a large window, her metal hand buried deep in the pocket of her jacket. She wasn’t really looking out, moreso staring down into the void between the stars that lingered at her feet, her mind spinning and adrenaline buzzing each time she inched her foot closer to the glass. A frown tugged at her lips, her mind wrestling with the restless thoughts over what she should send in her next communication.

The sound of heavy footsteps echoed down the corridor, breaking the stillness. She could tell by the hurried pace that it wasn’t a friendly visit, but she didn’t need to turn around to know who it was; the chittering and quick, irate breathing were unmistakable.

"Why are you out?" Sandy’s sharp voice cut through the silence, dripping with irritation as she approached.

Ramona didn’t immediately respond. She slowly tilted her head, just enough to glance at Sandy from the corner of her eye. Her expression darkened as she took note of the backup the Head of Security had brought, a wide-eyed cadet, clearly a new hire after the incident that took place a few months ago. And between the nervous shifting from one foot to the other and the way his hand hovered over his baton, she was sure Sandy had told him all sorts of horror stories about her. “What’s the problem this time? I couldn’t sleep and needed some fresh air.”

As Sandy opened her mouth to retort, Ramona’s vision began to blur. The purples of the galaxies and nebulas crept forward into the periphery of her sight, twisting and distorting the edges of Sandy’s face as a translucent, pink hand slowly oozed onto the woman’s shoulder. Ramona tilted her head down slightly, letting her bangs fall over her eyes to shield them from Sandy’s view, hiding the creeping chaos. Ramona then shifted her gaze back to the window, the frown deepening as she fought to regain control.

"You think you can just wander around? You're a liability, and you know it. The only reason you're still here is—"

“Because Pallas trusts me," Ramona interjected, her voice low but steady. She didn’t look back at Sandy, keeping her focus on the stars outside the window.

Sandy’s wings twitched again, her frustration growing. "Trust? That's a strong word for someone who’s—"

“Someone who’s what?” Ramona’s tone was icy now, her patience wearing thin. She could still see the colors, feel them twisting in her mind, but she couldn’t give the moth another reason towards locking her up. “Go on. Say it.”

The cadet shifted uncomfortably, his eyes darting between Sandy and Ramona, clearly unsure whether to intervene or stay silent. He finally settled on the latter, taking a half-step back.

Sandy hesitated, her wings twitching as she weighed her next words. Finally, she settled for a disdainful glare, her voice dropping to a near hiss. “Someone who’s far too dangerous to be left alone. You think you’re special because Pallas lets you play with his toys? You’re just his pawn, you always have been.”

“Aren’t we all his pawns?” She tilted her head back to watch Sandy fume from between her bangs. “I think I’m more like a bishop.”

“Good researchers don’t get turned into experiments.”

The cadet finally found his voice, though it was hesitant. “Chief, maybe we should—”

“Enough,” Sandy snapped, cutting him off. She glared at Ramona for a moment longer before turning on her heel, clearly deciding that she’d wasted enough time on her. “Get back to your room,” she threw over her shoulder as she stormed off, the cadet scurrying to keep up as her aggravated chitters echoed down the hall.

Ramona waited until their footsteps had faded into the distance before she allowed herself to lower herself into a crouch, her forehead pressing against the cool glass as she sighed, her eyes closing for a moment. She could feel the damp warmth of Iona sliding up behind her for a hug while she whispered scathing remarks about Sandy, but those comments went silently ignored.

As Ramona glanced out into space again, the sensations of her lover slowly faded away, leaving behind a lingering sense of unease.

Sol-68 (Checkmate)

Ramona watched idly as a borg zipped past through the halls while Pallas kept busy preparing a chessboard for them. It had only been a few days since she was able to stay awake for more than a few minutes at a time, but he was eager to play, having already moved his pawn to E4 to begin the game.

“Pawn, E5… When are you going to let me move again?”

He paused, glancing up at her before moving the piece for her. He set his bishop to C4. “I’m going as fast as I can, stardust. A few more days and you'll be breathing without that respirator and in a wheelchair by next week if all goes well. But we do need to talk about your arms.”

“Pawn, D5... I’m tired of this game…”

“I know,” he mumbled as he moved his queen to H5. “We’re giving you a choice this time. Cooperate with the experiments and you’ll get your arms back. Both of them.”

She frowned. “Knight to F6.”

His lips twitched at that, a ghost of amusement as he tapped his queen into place. “Queen to F7. That's checkmate, dear.” He leaned back with a reassuring smile, but she couldn’t see it in his eyes. “I can promise you that it won’t be like before. You’ll have a say in the…”

Her gaze flicked past him as the borg zipped by again, its movement precise, unyielding.
She could feel him watching her, waiting for some kind of reaction.
Instead, she rolled her shoulder as much as her body allowed and glanced back at the board. “Best two out of three, then.”

Sol-60 (Smoke in the Garden)

Ramona blinked slowly at the greenery before her, blurry colors sharpening into flowers and grass as her eye adjusted. But as her gaze roamed around, it paused on Pallas, who sat hunched over a table next to her stretcher, his knee bouncing and his fingers flicking some ash from his cigarette to the soil below. He flipped through pages in a binder.

“...patch,” she mumbled out, her voice raspy and muffled by an oxygen mask.

“Hm? Oh that… I replaced that marble with a proper glass eye. It’ll suffice until you’re well enough for a working replacement.”

“Oh…” She stared at the smoke curling up from his hand. “...want… cig…”

He snorted out a laugh, finally sitting up and turning to smirk at her. He said something, but she didn't hear. It was probably just something teasingly condescending anyways.
Her gaze drifted to his scars as he spoke. Two trailed down from his left eyebrow, one of them cutting through the corner of his lip. A third scar bisected the others, reaching up from the side of his jaw.

She had never asked about them. She wanted to, but her focus lingered on his lips, catching the motion as they formed that familiar two-syllable nickname before the weight of her eyelids pulled her back under.

Sol-56 (Recovery Surgery)

Glimpses of blue light filled her vision from time to time as she faded in and out of consciousness, but she couldn’t see much apart from the floor tiles swimming beneath her. Something pinched at her neck, inside her neck, but she couldn’t move. Pallas’ voice filtered in and out, and she could only catch segments of it as she shifted between states of lucidity. “You know… never did mean for this… happen to you… Poor stardust.”

Sol-64 (Fading)

The next time she opened her eye, she was staring up at the ceiling of his office. Her head felt like it was full of cotton, there was a respirator over her face, and everything sounded muffled, as if she were hearing something from underwater. Words phased in and out as she struggled to keep her eyes open. She couldn’t make out who he was talking to. “...moving… station… Leave… defects in the box… I don’t care… Alpha… report from 2X7A… shifting focus… glimmer affects the... But nothing is going to get done… functional again… Idiots at NanoTrasen… working with sticks and stones… at managing glimmer… never could have gotten… results… keeping it here… Some cooperation… Alpha is finally in order… She’s already blown two fucking holes in our station!” And then she finally noticed. Her eyepatch was gone.
